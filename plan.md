## 6. Multisystem Reasoning Framework

Teach a universal stepwise approach:

1. Identify systemic manifestations (fever, rash, hypotension, AMS, polyarthritis)  
2. Determine organ involvement (renal, respiratory, CNS, skin)  
3. Recognize characteristic lab/diagnostic clusters  
4. Establish likely disease category (infectious, autoimmune, endocrine, toxicologic)  
5. Apply evidence-based treatment sequencing  
6. Anticipate complications  
7. Evaluate need for escalation (ICU, airway, immunosuppressants)

Pitfalls:
- Misinterpreting rash clues  
- Missing autoimmune flare vs infection  
- Incorrect fluid choice in sepsis vs DKA  
- Overlooking toxic ingestion red flags  
- Delayed recognition of meningitis signs
